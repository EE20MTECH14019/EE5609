\section{Problem}
\\
In what ratio is the line joining \myvec{-1\\1} and \myvec{5\\7} is divided by the line
\begin{align}
\myvec{1 & 1}\vec{x} = 4
\end{align}
\section{Explanation}
The point \vec{X} divides the line segment joining the two points \vec{A}=\myvec{-1 \\ 1} and \vec{B}=\myvec{5 \\ 7} in ratio k : 1. Then,\\
\begin{align}
\vec{X}=\frac{(k\vec{B}+\vec{A})}{(k+1)}
\end{align}
\section{solution}
From the equation (2.0.1)
\begin{align}
(k+1)\vec{X} = k\vec{B}+\vec{A}
\end{align}
Let \vec{U}=\myvec{1\\1}
\begin{align}
\implies (k+1)\vec{U}^T\vec{X}=\vec{U}^T(k\vec{B}+\vec{A})\\
\implies k(\vec{U}^T\vec{X}-\vec{U}^T\vec{B})=\vec{U}^T\vec{A}-\vec{U}^T\vec{X}\\
\implies k=\frac{\vec{U}^T\vec{A}-\vec{U}^T\vec{X}}{\vec{U}^T\vec{X}-\vec{U}^T\vec{B}}
\end{align}
Hence on solving the equation (3.0.4) using
\begin{align}
\vec{U}^T\vec{X}=4
\end{align}
The line \myvec{1&1}\textbf{X}=4 divides the line joining points \textbf{A}=\myvec{-1\\1} and \textbf{B}=\myvec{5\\7} in the ratio k=1/2
